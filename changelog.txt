[2026-04-24] Drop zero-info constants, duplicate encoding, and NPI redundancy check
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Three further DROP_COLS additions and a new pre-training diagnostic cell:

1. 'Sex' and 'Cancer Type' removed — both are constant across the entire aligned
   cohort (all patients are Female / Breast Cancer). Constant features carry zero
   information and only add noise to the model and SHAP plots.

2. 'Oncotree Code' removed — it is a 1-to-1 re-encoding of 'Cancer Type Detailed'
   (e.g. IDC ↔ Breast Invasive Ductal Carcinoma). Keeping both gives that single
   piece of information twice the weight in tree splits and SHAP values. 'Cancer
   Type Detailed' is retained as the more human-readable form.

3. New cell added before training (section 3.4): prints the Pearson correlation of
   'Nottingham prognostic index' against its three component features — Tumor Size,
   Lymph nodes examined positive, and Neoplasm Histologic Grade (NPI formula:
   0.2 × size_cm + lymph_node_stage + grade). If the maximum component correlation
   exceeds 0.90 the cell automatically adds NPI to DROP_COLS and rebuilds the
   feature matrices, removing the near-perfectly-derived composite to avoid
   multicollinearity. On the current dataset the correlation is ~0.97, so NPI is
   dropped at runtime.

Re-run notebooks 03 → 04 → 05 after this change.

[2026-04-24] Drop 'Cohort' from feature set (confound removal)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Added 'Cohort' to DROP_COLS in the feature-selection cell.

'Cohort' (values 1, 2, 3) is a data-collection batch indicator from the METABRIC
patient history dataset (dataset2). It encodes which recruitment wave a patient
belonged to, not any clinical measurement. Because the three cohorts differ in
class distribution, the XGBoost model learned to exploit cohort membership as a
strong predictor of malignancy — an artefact confirmed by SHAP analysis (notebook
05_explainability.ipynb), where 'Cohort' appeared near the top of the mean |SHAP|
bar chart. Keeping it inflates apparent model performance and produces
clinically meaningless explanations. Dropping it forces the model to rely on
actual clinical and molecular features.

Re-run notebooks 03 → 04 → 05 after this change to update saved artefacts
(tabular_preprocess.joblib, tabular_xgb_model.json) and SHAP figures.

[2026-04-21] Drop post-treatment / outcome-proxy columns (leakage fix)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Moved 'Overall Survival Status', 'Overall Survival (Months)',
'Relapse Free Status', 'Relapse Free Status (Months)', 'Chemotherapy',
'Hormone Therapy', 'Radio Therapy', and 'Type of Breast Surgery' into a
dedicated LEAKAGE_COLS set and excluded them from feature_cols.

These columns are recorded after diagnosis and treatment decisions are made,
so including them would leak outcome information into the classifier and produce
unrealistically high metrics that would not generalise to a real screening
scenario.
